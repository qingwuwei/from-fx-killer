West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil has shown resilience, trading around $60.75 per barrel on Tuesday, reflecting a 1.20% increase. Traders are closely monitoring the situation as it unfolds, weighing it against ongoing supply concerns.

As the market reacts to these developments, the implications for the USD remain significant, particularly in relation to other currencies like the EUR. Strengthening oil prices can enhance USD value against its peers, influencing exchange rates and trading strategies.
